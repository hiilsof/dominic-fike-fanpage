Career & Background

Early Life

Profile


Photo by Ashley Olah

Full name: Dominic David Fike was born on December 30, 1995 in Naples, Florida of Filipino and African-American descent. Fike grew up playing music. By the age of ten , he had acquired a guitar and learned how to play it. Dominic Fike grew up with a younger brother, Alex, a sister, Apollonia, an older brother, Sean. His parents were frequently absent from his life, his mother was in and out of jail. As well as stating:

"Most of the male figures in my life were real sh*tty, People that I was supposed to call dad, they were real shitty to her [Mom], to us"


Source: Cancel Me Music Video (Dominic Fike with his younger sister "Apple")

And so he had to take care of his younger siblings, a brother and a sister. He also has two older brothers who sometimes took care of him. With no parents to take care of him as well as growing up around an unstable environment Fike spent most of his youth with his friend Max (often referring him to as "Max Flavor") who cooks simple, plain flavored meals for him because he has a sensitive diet.

“My stomach is real f*cked up. I did a bunch of coke when I was in high school, and after high school, and didn't eat as much as I should have. And it tore my stomach lining.”

Prison


Source: Florida Dept. of Corrections

Dominic Fike was arrested for battery in 2016. In 2017, he was sent to jail for violating his house arrest. And spent 2 months on house arrest before going to prison. Releasing his debut EP while still in jail. He made good use of his time in prison, saying that he had an interesting situation that spark a new way to write music.

“The sink would plays sounds. The sink had a tone, it had a note to it.”

Fike Experimented with different sounds like banging on the sink, finding a new source of writing music. He made it clear that the EP was not written inspired by the experience he had in jail, but noted that it did help the record's success.

Featured on Paul McCartney's Kiss of Venus


Youtube: Kiss of Venus

Kiss of Venus was included in McCartney III that was recorded in early 2020 at Paul McCartney's studio in Sussex, England while in lockdown during the COVID-19 pandemic. McCartney had this to say:

"It was a lot of fun. It was about making music for yourself rather than making music that has to do a job. So, I just did stuff I fancied doing. I had no idea this would end up as an album."

Available on all major streaming services on 16 April and was released in physical copy by 23 July. It includes Dominic Fike's version of "The Kiss of Venus", that was issued as its first single, as well as Beck's track and EOB's remix in other singles.
